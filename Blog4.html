Luxury Trains of India

Written by Mary Jane

Posted on February 11, 2025

India, known for its diverse landscapes and rich heritage, offers some of the most luxurious train journeys in the world. These trains provide travelers with a unique blend of royal grandeur, cultural richness, and world-class hospitality, offering an experience beyond just transportation. From the majestic Palaces on Wheels to the scenic routes showcasing India's heritage, these trains redefine luxury travel.

1. Maharajas’ Express: The Epitome of Royalty

Maharajas’ Express is considered one of the most luxurious trains in the world. Operated by the Indian Railway Catering and Tourism Corporation (IRCTC), it offers an experience fit for royalty , with opulent suites, gourmet dining, and personalized service.

Notable Features

  • Lavish Interiors– Inspired by the royal palaces of Rajasthan, the train has plush cabins with en-suite bathrooms.
  • Gourmet Dining– Two restaurants, Mayur Mahal and Rang Mahal, serve global and Indian delicacies.
  • Exclusive Lounges– Features a bar and lounge named Rajah Club for relaxation.

Popular Routes

  • The Indian Panorama– Covers Delhi, Jaipur, Ranthambore, Fatehpur Sikri, Agra, Orchha, Khajuraho, Varanasi, and Lucknow.
  • The Heritage of India– Explores Mumbai, Udaipur, Jodhpur, Bikaner, Jaipur, Ranthambore, and Agra.

2. Palace on Wheels: The Original Royal Experience

Launched in 1982, Palace on Wheels was India’s first luxury train, designed to recreate the opulence of royal carriages used by Rajput and British rulers.

Notable Features

  • Regal Coaches– Inspired by the princely states of Rajasthan, each coach is named after a former Rajput kingdom.
  • Fine Dining– Two onboard restaurants, Maharaja and Maharani, serve Rajasthani and global cuisine.
  • Luxury Amenities– Includes spa services, bars, and elegant lounges.

Popular Route

  • Covers Delhi, Jaipur, Sawai Madhopur (Ranthambore), Chittorgarh, Udaipur, Jaisalmer, Jodhpur, Bharatpur, and Agra.

3. The Deccan Odyssey: A Moving Palace

Inspired by the royal carriages of the Marathas, The Deccan Odyssey offers a luxurious exploration of Maharashtra and beyond . Often compared to the Orient Express, it is a premium way to experience India’s historical and cultural richness.

Notable Features

  • Elegant Cabins– Decorated with regal interiors reflecting Maharashtra’s royal history.
  • Onboard Spa– Provides rejuvenation therapies for travelers.
  • Business Facilities– Has a conference car, making it ideal for corporate travel.

Popular Routes

  • Maharashtra Splendor– Covers Mumbai, Nashik, Ajanta & Ellora Caves, Kolhapur, Goa, Sindhudurg, and Pune.
  • The Indian Sojourn– Explores Mumbai, Vadodara, Udaipur, Jodhpur, Agra, Ranthambore, Jaipur, and Delhi.

4. The Golden Chariot: Southern India in Style

Named after the Stone Chariot in Hampi, The Golden Chariot showcases the rich heritage of Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Kerala, and Goa. It is South India’s answer to the Palace on Wheels, offering world-class luxury with a touch of tradition.

Notable Features

  • Traditional Yet Modern– Each coach is named after dynasties like Hoysala, Vijayanagara, and Chalukya.
  • Ayurvedic Spa– The Arogya Spa offers relaxing Ayurvedic treatments.
  • Fine Dining– Two restaurants, Nalapaka and Ruchi, serve exquisite South Indian and continental dishes.

Popular Routes

  • Pride of Karnataka– Covers Bengaluru, Bandipur, Mysore, Halebidu, Chikmagalur, Hampi, and Goa.
  • Jewels of South– Explores Bengaluru, Mysore, Mahabalipuram, Thanjavur, Chettinad, Kochi, and Kumarakom.

5. The Fairy Queen Express: A Heritage Journey

The Fairy Queen Express is a unique luxury train pulled by the world’s oldest working steam locomotive , the Fairy Queen, built in 1855. It provides a blend of history and comfort.

Notable Features

  • Historic Locomotive– Recognized by the Guinness World Records as the oldest steam engine still in operation.
  • Intimate Experience– Accommodates a limited number of passengers for an exclusive journey.
  • Wildlife Adventure– The main attraction is a visit to Sariska Tiger Reserve.

Popular Route

  • Covers Delhi, Alwar, and Sariska Tiger Reserve.

6. The Buddhist Circuit Train: A Spiritual Luxury Journey

This luxury train is dedicated to Buddhist pilgrims, offering a spiritual tour across the major Buddhist heritage sites of India.

Notable Features

  • Meditation and Yoga– Designed for travelers seeking a peaceful retreat.
  • Buddhist-Inspired Interiors– Elegant decor reflecting Buddhist themes.
  • Curated Experiences– Includes guided tours to sacred sites.

Popular Route

  • Covers Bodh Gaya, Rajgir, Nalanda, Varanasi, Sarnath, Kushinagar, Lumbini (Nepal), and Sravasti.

Conclusion

India’s luxury trains redefine travel by combining heritage, comfort, and world-class hospitality. Whether you want to experience royal Rajasthan, scenic South India, or historic Maharashtra, these trains offer once-in-a-lifetime journeys. With palatial interiors, gourmet dining, and curated excursions, luxury train travel in India is an experience that transports passengers into a world of opulence and timeless elegance.
