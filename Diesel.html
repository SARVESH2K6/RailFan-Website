Diesel Locomotives

Diesel locomotives revolutionized Indian Railways, with workhorses like the WDM-2, WDP-4, and WDG-4 powering both passenger and freight services. While electric traction is replacing them in many regions, diesel engines remain crucial for remote routes, heavy haulage, and strategic operations across the country.

WDM-1

India’s first diesel workhorse, marking the transition from steam.

WDM-2

The backbone of Indian Railways, serving for over five decades.

WDM-3A

An upgraded version of WDM-2, built for better efficiency.

WDM-4

A short-lived diesel giant, later overshadowed by WDM-2.

WDS-1

A pioneer in Indian diesel shunting, built for station yards.

WDM-3D

A modernized diesel powerhouse for both freight and passenger use.

WDP-1

Built for speed, this locomotive was designed for express trains.

WDP-3A

A high-speed locomotive often seen pulling Rajdhani Express.

WDG-3A

A freight locomotive built to haul heavy loads across India.

WDP-4

India’s first indigenously built high-speed diesel locomotive.

WDG-4

A heavy-haul freight locomotive designed for long distances.

WDG-5

India's most powerful diesel locomotive, built for freight.

WDS-6

A powerful diesel shunter used in major railway yards.

WDG-6G

A modern freight locomotive built with American technology.

WDAP-5

An experimental locomotive blending diesel and electric power.
