Indian Railway Zones

The Indian Railways is divided into multiple zones, each playing a crucial role in managing railway operations across the country. These zones ensure smooth connectivity, efficient transportation of passengers and goods, and the seamless functioning of the vast railway network. From the bustling Northern Railway, which connects major cities, to the scenic Konkan Railway, offering breathtaking coastal routes, each zone has its own significance. This page provides insights into the history, key routes, major stations, and operational importance of every Indian Railway Zone, showcasing their role in shaping India's transportation system.

Northern Railway

The Northern Railway, established in 1952, is India's largest railway zone, covering key routes across Delhi, Punjab, and Uttar Pradesh.

Central Railway

Established in 1951, Central Railway connects Maharashtra, Madhya Pradesh, and beyond, with Mumbai as its headquarters.

Western Railway

Western Railway, headquartered in Mumbai, operates major routes across Gujarat, Maharashtra, Rajasthan, and Madhya Pradesh.

Eastern Railway

Eastern Railway, formed in 1952, covers key routes in West Bengal, Bihar, and Jharkhand, with Kolkata as its headquarters.

Southern Railway

Southern Railway, headquartered in Chennai, operates across Tamil Nadu, Kerala, Karnataka, and Andhra Pradesh, serving major coastal routes.

North Eastern Railway

Headquartered in Gorakhpur, North Eastern Railway plays a crucial role in connecting Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, and neighboring regions.

South Eastern Railway

South Eastern Railway, headquartered in Kolkata, is vital for freight and passenger transport across West Bengal, Jharkhand, and Odisha.

Northeast Frontier Railway

Headquartered in Maligaon, Assam, Northeast Frontier Railway connects the northeastern states with the rest of India, traversing challenging terrains.

East Central Railway

Established in 2002, East Central Railway, headquartered in Hajipur, Bihar, manages key rail routes across Bihar, Jharkhand, and Uttar Pradesh.

East Coast Railway

Headquartered in Bhubaneswar, East Coast Railway primarily serves Odisha, Andhra Pradesh, and Chhattisgarh, playing a key role in freight transport.

North Central Railway

Known as the "Gateway to the North," North Central Railway, headquartered in Prayagraj, manages major routes across Uttar Pradesh, Madhya Pradesh, and Rajasthan.

North Western Railway

Formed in 2002, North Western Railway, headquartered in Jaipur, connects Rajasthan, Haryana, Punjab, and Gujarat with its vast rail network.

South Central Railway

Headquartered in Secunderabad, South Central Railway plays a crucial role in connecting Telangana, Andhra Pradesh, Maharashtra, and Madhya Pradesh.

South Western Railway

Headquartered in Hubballi, South Western Railway covers Karnataka and parts of Tamil Nadu, Andhra Pradesh, and Goa.

Metro Railway Kolkata

Metro Railway Kolkata is India's only metro zone, operating the oldest metro system in the country since 1984.

West Central Railway

Formed in 2003, West Central Railway is headquartered in Jabalpur and operates in Madhya Pradesh and Rajasthan.

South East Central Railway

South East Central Railway, headquartered in Bilaspur, is a key freight carrier serving Chhattisgarh, Odisha, and Maharashtra.

South Coast Railway (SCoR)

Headquartered in Visakhapatnam, the South Coast Railway serves the coastal regions of Andhra Pradesh and Odisha, connecting key ports and cities.
